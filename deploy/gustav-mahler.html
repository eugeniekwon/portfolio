Gustav Mahler

— Typography, 2020 / Adobe Illustrator

  • Objective —

    To create a typography poster for a hypothetical concert that portrays the composer and the concert piece effectively

  • About —

    Gustav Mahler was one of the major composers of his generation, whose style reflected a blend of Romanticism and Modernism. He was a part of the Vienna Secession, a movement which emphasized the characteristics of expressionism. His composition “Symphony No. 5” starts off more in a minor key, which transitions into a major key; it is considered a marching piece. Out of the five movements, the fourth movement is an exception in that it is the only part that establishes a calm feeling from a series of sustained notes; it is also special due to the fact that it was inspired by his wife, Alma Schindler. The piece ends with a similar momentum that was reflected in previous movements. In its overall shift from minor to major keys, it is also a work that stands as a representation of the trajectory of his personal life.

  • Process —

    To portray Mahler’s march, a dynamic scale shift was included to reflect the juxtaposition of both majestic and calming features. There is also a repetition of similar musical motifs, which is represented with the visual motifs of similar shapes at the bottom of the poster. The main poster shows the dark version especially because the entire piece stays in the minor key. Because Mahler was a part of the Vienna Secession, the overall style of the poster was also influenced by the distinctive artist style of this movement, developed among the four major artists and musicians (one of them being Gustav Klimt).
