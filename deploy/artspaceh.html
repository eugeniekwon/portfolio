Artspace H

— Brand Identity + Rebranding, 2021 / Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, After Effects, Sketch

  • Objective —

    To create a new brand identity To renew the official website and expand the social media presence that is more visual and informative

  • About —

    Artspace H provides unique exhibitions to the general public, participates in diverse art fairs, and assists art collectors and visitors with their needs. They discover and support rising artists while they put even more focus on planning and directing a variety of exhibitions. Artspace H emphasizes on the H: humanity, harmony, and happiness.

  • Process —

    This is the first rebrand that the gallery is going through ever since it opened. One of the major problems was that the previous logo did not fulfill the current gallery’s mission. Since the gallery is focusing on providing unique exhibitions, the solution to this idea was to use folding walls to create the flexibility of the gallery in a pictorial mark (with the hidden H). The folding wall shows importance when it comes to creating a valuable and comfortable space for viewers; it is flexible to create different spaces and routes depending on each exhibition. Since the folding wall is not constrained to a particular orientation, it matches with the gallery’s mission and values of being flexible and creative with the exhibitions that they create and the various art fairs that they enter. In addition, it was not necessary to use the words “gallery” and “artspace” together since they essentially share the same meaning. As Artspace H marked its 14th year anniversary, it was still missing the modern and professional aspect compared to other galleries. On the other hand, it is continuing with the emphasis of the H of humanity, harmony, and happiness. In order to contain the values of the H and the modern aspect all in one, the wordmark was created to have the rounded corners of the counter spaces and sharp corners outside of the letters. In addition, there needed to be a major change to the Instagram page (and other social media pages) as well as the official website. There were revisions to the copywriting and the organization/layout, which creates more effectiveness on providing information. Posts and updates will be more visually engaging than before (especially with upcoming and current exhibitions).
