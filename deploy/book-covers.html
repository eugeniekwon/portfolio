Orlando / White Teeth

— Book Cover Design, 2021 / Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop

  • Objective —

    To create two book covers, one classical and one contemporary book, with given dimensions

  • About —

    Orlando is a classic book by Virginia Woolf that centers around a nobleman Orlando where he goes through a series of events, and eventually becomes a woman; Orlando then experiences the life of a mother and wife, which reflects the shift in gender roles in the 18th and 19th century of England. White Teeth is a contemporary book by Zadie Smith that focuses on the story of three families in London after the war. Archie Jones and Samad Iqbal are two families that are first introduced where they later interact with a different family which eventually creates a chaotic and comical series of events while showing the cultural aspects of each.

  • Process —

    For Orlando, there was a different idea of using the oak tree as main imagery derived from one of the important symbols that the book addressed. Yet, the idea of using the name “Orlando” as typographic, geometric shapes was applied to the front and back of the cover to best portray the plot. To show the shift of gender that Orlando experiences, the columns of typography (either basing it off of existing fonts or creating from scratch) are manipulated and are placed either on black or on white background. Each column uses the same set of colours on two backgrounds to show how Orlando’s internal characteristics and personality would remain constant (and room for growth) even if external characteristics changed (typographic forms change); the concept of colour illusion is applied where even the same set of colours would appear differently on different backgrounds. For White Teeth, there were some experiments done with physical materials, however, the direction towards digital illustration became the final direction. Incorporating teeth was an idea that started off with sketching. At one point, the idea of removing a few teeth occurred as one of the characters does not have a full set of teeth, and it depicts the comical aspect of the novel; it also serves as an important symbolism in the novel where there is a societal standard/norm of having a full set of white teeth. Using dynamic handwritten typography compliments the illustration.
